# Title Writing Guidelines

> Agent-facing guide for `/writer`. Paper titles, section headings, and title review checklist.

---

## 1. Paper Title

### DO

- **Accurately describe content**: Reflect research topic and method
- **Be concise**: 10–15 words; ≤80 characters
- **Front-load keywords**: Search engines prioritize first 6–7 words
- **Use specific terms**: AVOID generic ("A New Method") → USE technique name ("Geodesic Imputation")
- **Use prepositions**: *via*, *for*, *through*, *with* to connect concepts
- **Optional subtitle**: Colon-separated; adds method or scope (e.g., *BRITS: Bidirectional Recurrent Imputation for Time Series*)

### AVOID

- **Filler openings**: "Study on...", "Research of...", "Analysis of..."
- **Empty adjectives**: "novel", "new", "innovative", "advanced"
- **Questions**: Unless venue explicitly allows
- **Definite claims**: "The Optimal Method for..." (implies absoluteness)
- **Unexpanded abbreviations**: Spell out or avoid in title
- **Excessive length**: >15 words → simplify

## 2. Section Headings

### Level Conventions

- **L1** (1–3 words): Noun phrase. *Introduction*, *Method*, *Experiments*
- **L2** (2–5 words): Noun phrase or gerund. *Problem Statement*, *Metric Learning*
- **L3** (3–6 words): Specific technical point. *Cholesky Parameterization*, *Geodesic Distance Approximation*

### Style Rules (NeurIPS/ICML/ICLR 2023–2025)

- **Front-load core concept**: Key term at heading start
- **No questions**: Section headings almost never use question marks
- **No colon explanations**: AVOID "Motivation: Why Riemannian Metrics" → USE "Metric Learning Motivation"
- **Be specific**: AVOID "Problem and Motivation" → USE concrete technical content
- **Parallel structure**: Same-level headings share grammatical form

## 3. Title Review Checklist (T1–T10)

1. **T1**: Core keywords present in first half of title?
2. **T2**: No filler opening ("Study on", "Research of", "Analysis of")?
3. **T3**: No empty adjectives ("novel", "new", "innovative")?
4. **T4**: Concise? (Paper title ≤15 words; section heading ≤8 words)
5. **T5**: Specific? (Names the technique, not "A Method for...")
6. **T6**: Parallel structure? (Same-level headings grammatically consistent)
7. **T7**: No unexpanded abbreviations?
8. **T8**: No question marks?
9. **T9**: Important terms front-loaded?
10. **T10**: Subtitle justified? (Only if it adds method/scope info; omit if unnecessary)
